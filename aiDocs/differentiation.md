## NestEgg's Differentiators

### 1. The only product at the birth moment with multi-account breadth
Every competitor either targets the birth moment (Fabric) or covers multiple financial products (Greenlight) — but none do both. NestEgg owns the intersection.

### 2. Guidance-first, not account management
Competitors that do cover investing (UNest, Acorns Early, Greenlight) are account management platforms — they hold or manage the money. NestEgg is purely guidance: it walks parents to third-party providers and gets out of the way. This means:
- No regulatory burden (no RIA registration required)
- No custody or fiduciary responsibility
- No switching cost for the user — they own the accounts, not NestEgg

### 3. The trifecta is the product
The 529 + UTMA + authorized user combination is not a feature list — it's a deliberate, opinionated strategy that no competitor has framed as a package. Each account solves a different risk:
- 529: optimized for education
- UTMA: backstop if education path changes
- Authorized user: credit history that no investment account can provide

### 4. The projection hook is the acquisition mechanism
The interactive projection card — visible before any signup, no login required — is not just a feature. It is the reason a parent clicks "Set this up." Competitors either bury the outcome (state 529 portals show you an application, not a dollar number) or require signup before showing value (Greenlight, UNest).

## Where NestEgg Is Weak

Honest assessment of what competitors do better:

| Weakness | Who does it better | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Brand trust | Greenlight (established), Fidelity | Parents may trust a big name over an unknown startup |
| Account management after setup | Greenlight, UNest | NestEgg has no ongoing relationship — parents may disengage |
| Mobile-first UX | Greenlight (native app) | NestEgg is web-only until Phase 5 |
| Gifting experience | Backer | Backer's gifting UX is purpose-built; NestEgg's is a side feature |

These weaknesses inform the Phase 4–6 roadmap: mobile app (Phase 5), contribution reminders (Phase 3), and deeper gifting (Phase 4).
